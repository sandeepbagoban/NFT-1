YokaiVerse

YokaiVerse is a collection of 9,000 generative Yokais with hundred of elements inspired by the Japan Pop-Culture.

Each artwork is original, with its own color palette and creation. The objective was to make each Yokai unique in order to prioritize quality above quantity.

  • Sutoku Tennō

    Emperor Sutoku, feared in life, more so in death, is one of the most famous Yokai to haunt Japan. Unyielding, undying, his body did not decay after his death, and when he was finally cremated his ashes formed a formidable cloud in the sky, mingling with the storm that heralded the end of his physical body. The bringer of storms, plagues, etc., Sutoku is the harbinger of disaster - claiming vengeance on those who wronged him in life, and their descendants. He still seeks to claim Japan for himself, seeing it as rightfully is, even in death, and he continues to haunt the country as one of the ‘Three Great Onryō’.

  • Jorōgumo

    A solitary creature in the shape of a stunningly beautiful woman, the Jorōgumo entices her victims with her feminine wiles; which is why her name translates to ‘entangling bride’ or ‘whore spider’ as was the original meaning. Cunning, intelligent and ruthless, the Jorōgumo view humans as little more than flies to entice into their webs to feed upon, especially foolish men, and women in this more modern society. A gifted shapeshifter, she uses her ability to disguise herself as a woman to attract her prey, ensnaring them in her silk threads stronger than steel, allowing her to slowly devour her meal with her powerful venom. She is also able to summon thousands of fire-breathing spiders, should she need to rid herself of any meddling fools wishing to try and harm her.

  • Yūrei

    Yūrei are as unique as the deceased person they came from. Upon death the Yūrei retain the features and clothing the person had upon their death, though their clothes and features may look more disheveled in their now translucent forms. For those who died a particularly bloody death, their wounds will be apparent as well, but all Yūrei hang their heads in such a way that their hair obscures their faces, and they glide through graveyards or houses, or other places near to where they died, with the hands held limply before them, no feet visible as they glide about, haunting one particular place or person, usually where they died, a particular loved one or possibly the person who killed them. Yūrei may look harmless, if a little frightening, but they are capable of powerful curses, and they tend to remain if they have something left over from life that needs to be achieved - their killer brought to justice, or passing a message to a loved one, for others they simply cannot accept their own demise.

  • Daitengu

    The Greater Tengu often have their own individual names which they prefer to go by, they appear more human-like though they still sport the Tengu’s typically red face and phallic nose - the longer the nose, the more powerful the Tengu. Dressed as monks, with large feathered wings, they can transform into an avian form but choose to do so less often than the lesser Tengu. They are a solitary creature, spending most of their time meditating and focussing on their intellect, which makes them all the more dangerous. Extremely powerful and savage, they can be just as unpredictable as other Tengu, but coupled with their wisdom they are far more terrifying adversaries. They are, however, more restrained and give worthy human’s aid when asked, seen more as wise sages than the monsters their lesser, more aggressive brethren are often seen as.

  • Oni

    Oni are some of the more well known Yokai. These huge beasts, often translated as ogre or demon, stand taller than most men and come in many varieties, though the most well known have either red or blue skin and shocking, wild black hair with horns jutting from their heads, and huge yellowing fang-like tusks that overlap their mouths. The number of horns, eyes, fingers and toes differ from Oni to Oni, but all wear loincloths made from the pelts of great beasts slaughtered by their own hands. They possess great strength and stamina, and despite their appearance are in fact accomplished sorcerers. They are known to punish the damned in Hell, where they reside or in caves on mountainsides, ready to find new victims. They are created when truly wicked humans die, and are transformed into Oni in Hell, to punish and create new Oni when new souls arrive. They are well known for being villainous creatures that bring about many natural disasters.

  • Kappa

    Appearing as reptilian-humanoids, these ‘river children’ are most at home in the water. They appear much like children in size, but don’t be fooled by their small stature, they are as strong as any grown man. With scaled skin ranging from deep, earthy green to bright red or blue, the Kappa’s webbed feet are built for swimming through the water at high speeds. With a shell on their backs and a turtle-like beak, their appearance is often preceded by the intense smell of rotting fish due to their elasticated skin, which can apparently be removed. The tops of their heads dip inward like a dish, and as they appear above the surface it collects water, this strange dish is the source of their power - should the water be spilled or dry up, they will be rendered immobile or possibly even die. As young they remain in groups, but as adults they become more solitary, though they are not opposed to pairing with other Yokai or people. They are extremely intelligible, and can speak any language; if you can befriend one they can be loyal and loving, if mischievous and their proud natures mean they will never break a promise. Kappa are also known for becoming violent, however, taking their mischief too far and drowning people in order to eat them, on land you may be able to outwit a Kappa into bowing to you in order to spill their water, but in water you’re doomed.

  • Tanuki

    These mischievous ‘racoon dogs’ are extremely powerful, magical beings. They are extremely agile, and well known for their cheerful, jovial nature and love of playing pranks on people. Able to shape-shift at will, they love to change into people or other creatures in order to play their tricks. The oddest thing about a Tanuki is their massive testicles, which they can manipulate at will and adapt to whatever they require - they are known for creating fishing nets, umbrellas, drums, fans and even weapons from them and their testicles are usually incorporated into their disguises. Should they become a shopkeeper, it is their testicles that become the shop or a cart. They are not a vicious creature, they are playful by nature and often viewed as cute pranksters who mean little to no harm in reality. That’s not to say a few haven’t turned from the playful pranks to more malicious ones on occasion, but their love of human life such as gambling and drinking means they don’t tend to want ill-will pushed their way.

  • Kitsune

    Not to be mistaken for a common fox, the Kitsune are extremely powerful magic beings. More commonly known for being mischief makers, the wild foxes are famous for their pranks, they like to trick or possess humans for fun, causing them to act strangely. Despite the wild foxes' somewhat evil nature, even they keep their promises and remember friendships, they all repay their debts. The lesser known Kitsune are the Holy foxes, servants of the Shinto deity Inari. They protect the shrines and act as messengers for the gods, protecting humans and places while providing good luck and keeping away evil spirits. Almost the polar opposite of their wild fox kin. Extremely intelligent and powerful, Kitsune are known for punishing the wicked, creating horrible nightmares to scare, or tricking them into humiliating situations as punishment. Some Kitsune prefer to spend their time in a human form, on occasion they have been known to accidentally wed a human and live a fairly ordinary life, only really exposed when drunk or startled into revealing their fangs or a patch of fur. Though most prefer to stick to their mischievous nature, earning all foxes (magical or otherwise) a reputation for trouble.

  • Hunter

    Where most humans are unable to see Yokai, until it’s too late, this particular clan have been blessed with the ability to see them, and also gives them the skills to exterminate the Yokai. The clan are little more than a rumour, operating from the shadows to protect the rest of the world from the Yokai menace. They are all women; an apprentice is usually taken into the clan when a Master sees a girl with the potential to hunt Yokai. The girls are trained, taught to sense the various Yokai, and how to kill each one. They are ranked based on their abilities, receiving a magatama to show how powerful they are, starting with one before becoming a Master with three. They are required to take part in rituals, drinking a powerful potion that helps them to acquire new skills that aid them in destroying their foes. Similar to the Samurai, the Hunters follow a set code of honour; those who go against this are quickly shunned from the clan.
